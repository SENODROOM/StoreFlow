# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences**
- **Gracefully accepting constructive criticism**
- **Focusing on what is best for the community**
- **Showing empathy towards other community members**

### Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind**
- **Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks**
- **Public or private harassment**
- **Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission**
- **Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting**

## Enforcement Responsibilities

### Project Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, and it also applies when an individual is representing the project or its community in public spaces. Examples of representing a project or community include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you witness or are subject to unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please contact the project team immediately. You can reach us through:

- **Email**: [conduct@example.com](mailto:conduct@example.com)
- **GitHub Issues**: Create a new issue with the "Code of Conduct" label
- **Direct Message**: Contact any maintainer directly

### What to Include in Your Report

When making a report, please include:

- Your contact information (optional, but helpful for follow-up)
- The specific behavior that was inappropriate
- The time and place where the incident occurred
- Any relevant context or additional information
- Names of individuals involved (if known and comfortable sharing)

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with confidentiality and sensitivity. We will not share your report with anyone without your explicit permission, except as necessary to investigate and resolve the issue.

## Response Process

### Initial Response

- **Acknowledgment**: We will acknowledge receipt of your report within 24 hours
- **Assessment**: We will assess the report to determine appropriate action
- **Communication**: We will keep you informed about the progress and outcome

### Investigation

- **Thorough Review**: We will conduct a thorough and impartial investigation
- **Evidence Gathering**: We will collect relevant evidence and statements
- **Timeline**: Most investigations will be completed within 7 days

### Resolution

- **Action**: We will take appropriate action based on the investigation findings
- **Follow-up**: We will follow up with all parties involved
- **Documentation**: We will document the incident and resolution for future reference

## Consequences

### Possible Actions

Unacceptable behavior from any community member, including sponsors and those with decision-making authority, will not be tolerated. Anyone asked to stop unacceptable behavior is expected to comply immediately.

Depending on the severity and nature of the behavior, consequences may include:

- **Warning**: A formal warning about the behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from all project spaces
- **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement for illegal activities

### Appeal Process

If you believe a consequence was unfairly applied, you may appeal by:

1. Contacting the project maintainers within 7 days of the consequence
2. Providing a detailed explanation of why you believe the consequence was unfair
3. Including any relevant evidence or context
4. The maintainers will review the appeal within 5 days

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Standards

#### In Discussions and Issues
- **Be constructive**: Focus on solutions rather than problems
- **Stay on topic**: Keep discussions relevant to the project
- **Be patient**: Remember that community members have different schedules and time zones
- **Use clear language**: Avoid jargon and explain technical concepts when necessary

#### In Code Reviews
- **Be specific**: Provide clear, actionable feedback
- **Be kind**: Remember that code reviews are about improving the code, not criticizing the person
- **Explain your reasoning**: Help others understand your perspective
- **Be open to discussion**: Be willing to reconsider your own suggestions

#### In Pull Requests
- **Follow contribution guidelines**: Ensure your PR meets all requirements
- **Write clear descriptions**: Explain what your changes do and why
- **Respond to feedback**: Address review comments promptly and professionally
- **Keep discussions focused**: Stay on topic during review conversations

### Collaboration Principles

#### Working Together
- **Assume good intent**: Believe that others are trying to help
- **Be inclusive**: Welcome newcomers and help them learn
- **Share knowledge**: Help others grow and learn
- **Respect different perspectives**: Value diverse opinions and experiences

#### Conflict Resolution
- **Address issues directly**: Talk to the person involved first
- **Seek mediation**: Ask maintainers for help if needed
- **Focus on the issue**: Separate the problem from the person
- **Find common ground**: Look for mutually acceptable solutions

## Specific Guidelines for Different Roles

### For Contributors

#### Before Contributing
- **Read the documentation**: Understand the project structure and guidelines
- **Check existing issues**: Avoid duplicating work
- **Start small**: Begin with good first issues or documentation improvements
- **Ask questions**: If you're unsure about anything, ask for clarification

#### During Contribution
- **Follow coding standards**: Adhere to the project's style guidelines
- **Write tests**: Ensure your changes are properly tested
- **Update documentation**: Keep documentation up to date with your changes
- **Be responsive**: Respond to review comments in a timely manner

### For Maintainers

#### Responsibilities
- **Lead by example**: Demonstrate the behavior you expect from others
- **Be fair and consistent**: Apply rules equally to all community members
- **Provide guidance**: Help contributors understand and follow guidelines
- **Protect the community**: Take action against unacceptable behavior

#### Decision Making
- **Be transparent**: Explain your decisions and reasoning
- **Seek input**: Consider community feedback when making decisions
- **Document decisions**: Keep records of important decisions and their rationale
- **Be accountable**: Take responsibility for your decisions and their impact

### For New Community Members

#### Getting Started
- **Introduce yourself**: Let the community know who you are
- **Start with small contributions**: Build confidence and understanding
- **Ask for help**: Don't hesitate to ask questions
- **Learn from others**: Observe how experienced contributors interact

#### Growing in the Community
- **Take on more responsibility**: Gradually increase your involvement
- **Mentor others**: Help newcomers when you have experience
- **Share your knowledge**: Contribute to discussions and documentation
- **Provide feedback**: Help improve the project and community
